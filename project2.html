Ok Dive

A solo project done for an online UI/UX Design competition in Summer 2023.
The purpose of the project was to create something that you would be proud of and come up with design quickly.
The design competition was a rapid fire one which only lasted for 4 days.
I decided to make an app related to scuba diving as it seemed something I would want to do in the future.

Prototype

Stages

Design Brief

OKDive is a diving app concept that aims to bring a more convenient way for free divers to get prepared for their diving journey.

Inital Concept

The first concept I had in mind was making a app which acts like a living encyclopedia as it would give information about the aquatic life taht you might experience when you were doing scuba diving.

Final Design

But after so many considerations and realizing it was too ambitious and would take too long to make it, I decided to go with something simpler and maybe more useful too. Ok Dive which is basically a diving companion app.

Understanding Scuba:

  • The process of planning requires careful consideration of factors such as weather, tides, diving partner, and more... each categories takes great efforts to plan. I came to wonder, how can we improve diving process? What is so hard about dive planning? With this question in mind, I conducted interviews with professional freedivers to gain insight into their experiences and discover ways in which the diving process could be improved
  • Challenges: One of the main challenges making this app was I had basically zero experience related to scuba diving and had no idea

Design Process

Unique and Cohesive Design:
I wanted to designed the app in such a way that the users would understand what to do just by looking. For the home page, I added affordances such as the image bleeding of the page to give users an indication that more content is present there and they can swipe it, same with the today section at the bottom of the page. I also added a “+” button for adding family members which can be easily accessed and adding people from there is pretty simple too.

Final Design

  • This was the final design that was prototyped in ProtoPie.
  • We used a lot of affordances, to reduce the mental stress from, the seniors so they have an easier time navigating through the app without much trouble.
  • The most challenging part was actually finding seniors to test and debug the app, but we someone managed to pull through as one of the teammate had family visiting over and they helped us in the testing.

Key Takeaways:

  • Interactive Design: I deepened my understanding of interaction design, especially in handling complex layouts and making sure things are not too complex so we can also achieve accessibility.
  • Team Dynamics: Working in a team taught me valuable lessons in communication and collaborative problem-solving.

Challenges and Resolutions:

  • The initial challenges that we faced was having problems with how to make an interafce simple for seniors, but a lot of iterations and ideas later, also a lot of feedback from the teaching team helped us reach a final design that we are proud of.
  • Last challenge was making the actual working prototype as it was the first time I was using ProtoPie so it had a learning curve and some interactions were complex when compared to figma but overtime and with the help of tutorials I was able to overcome this challenge.
