Kin - Connect

This was a team project for IAT334 Fall 2023, this project was done in teams of 3. The project’s goal was to develop an app for seniors who want to connect with their family and loved ones.
I was the lead UI/UX designer for this project, and came with the name “kin” for the app.

Prototype

Stages

Ideation

In the beginning, we had the challenge of selecting a relevant domain that would fit the needs of our user groups and such, so we did a lot of ideation and then finally decided to go with a social media for seniors.

Figma

After the initial stages and ideas, when me and my team were ready to move to the next stage, we decided to use Figma as it allowed us to work together and get the wireframes ready faster for the final stage.

Protopie

After we were done with Figma wireframes, I moved to ProtoPie to implement all the wireframes and get the desired interactions for the app, and test it with pilot interviews with different groups of people.

Initial Process

  • After having the initial feedback from the TAs on how the idea is coming along, I went ahead and made initial sketches for the app’s interface to get further feedback from the teaching team and move forward with this idea.
  • With the choice of a warm color orange, I came up with this designs while my teammates created their own designs. I wanted to incorporate design principles I follow so I came up with this design.
  • Challenges: One of the core challenge was that the initial design might have been too bright for the seniors, so we had to overcome that and I decided to use a shade of blue as after the interviews with seniors, I discovered they like blue color more than the orange and we decided to go with that moving forward.

Design Process

Unique and Cohesive Design:
I wanted to designed the app in such a way that the users would understand what to do just by looking. For the home page, I added affordances such as the image bleeding of the page to give users an indication that more content is present there and they can swipe it, same with the today section at the bottom of the page. I also added a “+” button for adding family members which can be easily accessed and adding people from there is pretty simple too.

Final Design

  • This was the final design that was prototyped in ProtoPie.
  • We used a lot of affordances, to reduce the mental stress from the seniors so they have an easier time navigating through the app without much trouble.
  • The most challenging part was actually finding seniors to test and debug the app, but we someone managed to pull through as one of the teammate had family visiting over and they helped us in the testing.

Key Takeaways:

  • Interactive Design: I deepened my understanding of interaction design, especially in handling complex layouts and making sure things are not too complex so we can also achieve accessibility.
  • Team Dynamics: Working in a team taught me valuable lessons in communication and collaborative problem-solving.

Challenges and Resolutions:

  • The initial challenges that we faced was having problems with how to make an interafce simple for seniors, but a lot of iterations and ideas later, also a lot of feedback from the teaching team helped us reach a final design that we are proud of.
  • Last challenge was making the actual working prototype as it was the first time I was using ProtoPie so it had a learning curve and some interactions were complex when compared to figma but overtime and with the help of tutorials I was able to overcome this challenge.
